### –ò—Å—Ç–æ—Ä–∏—è













**Go** was designed at Google in **2007** to improve <u>programming productivity</u> in an era of multicore, networked machines and large codebases.



Go was publicly announced in November **2009**, and version **1.0** was released in March **2012**. Go is widely used in production at Google and in many other organizations and <u>open-source</u> projects.



**Robert "Rob" C. Pike** (born **1956**) is a Canadian üá®üá¶ programmer and author. He is best known for his work on the Go programming language and at <u>Bell Labs</u>, where he was a member of the <u>Unix</u> team.



**Kenneth Lane Thompson**  (born **1943**) is an American üá∫üá∏ pioneer of computer science. Thompson worked at <u>Bell Labs</u> for most of his career where he designed and implemented the original <u>Unix</u> operating system. Since **2006**, Thompson has worked at Google, where he co-invented the Go programming language.
